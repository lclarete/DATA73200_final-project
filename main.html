Relevance

New York City is multiethnic and diverse city. It has a long history of immigration from outside and is the biggest metropolis in the US.


Over 8.3 million people called New York City home in 2019, making up more than 40% of the population of New York State and a somewhat smaller portion of the 23.6 million-person New York metropolitan region.


Despite being one of the richest cities in the world, it's still a highly unequal city. The inequality is linked to a few variables, such as geography, gender and ethnicity.

The Social and Economic Inequality dashboard displays a map and chart of the percent of people in each zip code comparing both populations who receives social benefits versus the on who don't. Also showing the difference between residents of different ethnicities.


Users can select their own zip code to view detailed data about socio demographic differences in both zip codes. The dashboard shows socio demographic data for two equal groups, which is useful for increasing awareness about inequality within an area and connecting this with wider issues.
